Matching Market Examples

2019/04/12

A matching market is one in which parties have to be personally matched to each other because they have preferences about whom they transact with; a market price alone is insufficient to determine who should interact with whom. Examples include employees & firms, graduate students & advisors, and romantic partners.

Another example is professional collaborators - e.g. scientists or artists whose interests and skills complement each other. Of the three examples above, one (graduate students & advisors) features few options available to both parties. But the other two are large markets, and large companies have been created to facilitate the matching - LinkedIn, Tinder, etc.

Are there similar tools for ‘independent agents’ - academics, writers, entrepreneurs, artists, etc. to meet and work with others like them? If not, it seems like there’s a big opportunity here.

Another example: matching research methods and techniques to interesting questions and problems. This ‘market’ is constantly running in the brain of every academic.
